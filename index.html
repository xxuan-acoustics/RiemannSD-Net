§ 2

Why Disentanglement Matters

The same hard case — same speaker, different TTS system — processed by three models. The baseline is misled by shared speaker identity; our methods focus on synthesis traces.

AAM-Softmax
Baseline
Score: 0.67 Predicts "Same Source" — above threshold
✗ Wrong
Ground truth: Different
ChebySD-AAM
Ours
Score: 0.43 Predicts "Different Source" — below threshold
✓ Correct
EER ↓ 1.39%
RiemanSD-AAM
Ours · Best
Score: 0.31 Predicts "Different Source" — clearly below threshold
✓ Correct
EER ↓ 2.64%
Key insight: The baseline's score (0.67) is inflated by speaker similarity — the same voice is cloned by both systems. ChebySD-AAM uses Chebyshev polynomial gradient stabilization to resist this, and RiemanSD-AAM additionally projects embeddings into hyperbolic space where speaker and source distributions are geometrically separated.

Evaluation Protocols

Four protocols designed to independently stress-test source discrimination (seen/unseen generators) and speaker disentanglement (same/different speaker). Each is balanced to a 1:1 positive-negative ratio.
